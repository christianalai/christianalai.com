Fare Families

A feature that the Flights application lacked was allowing the customers to choose between different fare families (Basic Economy, Main Cabin, First Class, etc.) for their flight itinerary. The goal here was to inform the customer of what amenities were included in their current package as well as show them what was being offered in the other packages.

So that the module didn’t disrupt the existing experience and user flow, the Fare Families module was designed to be inside the preexisting “Offers” tab with the different fares being displayed as pre-collapsed buckets.

Each fare serves as a button so users can click comfortably on all devices.

Seat Selection

To create the best overall Flights experience, we had to solve for one of our customers’ greatest pain points— the seat selection experience. Previously they were only able to select seats on the desktop browser and they were inundated with useless information and just confused by the overall selection process. So up until this redesign, there wasn’t a responsive experience with a mobile-first approach.

In this revamped experience, we did away with information that wasn't necessary to the customer's decision-making and streamlined the process.

In the previous experience, users were confused by where they were in the selection path.

In the updated experience, we use clear visual indicators to specify who they're choosing the flights for and for which leg of the itinerary.
