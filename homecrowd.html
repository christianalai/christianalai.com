Homecrowd

Overview

Homecrowd is a side project developed by an associate that I’ve undertaken as a conceptual project to hone my UX design skillset. The aim of this project is to create a directory of bars to connect fans with venues that will be showing the game where other loyal fans will be.

Role

For this project, I was responsible for creating the new look and feel through competitive research, ideation, sketching, wireframing, prototyping, and testing.

Tools used: Sketch, Invision

Competitive Analysis

During my research into other information sources, I gathered that though Yelp and Google provided relevant results, it required substantial perusing and click-throughs to ultimately get the information. Team Bar Finder is an unknown source but it did give the most relevant information, however, it also required some amount of clicking and scrolling to retrieve the relevant details. This diagram is a high-level example of a comparison matrix.

User Research

To understand who I was designing for, I conducted 1-on-1 interviews to deduce what the main concerns were and to understand what users generally look for when it comes to discovering new places to go. Through these interviews, I found out some key insights:

  • - when it came to sports bars, the main concern was having enough TVs, seating, and good beer/food selection (in this order)
  • - when it came to finding sports bars while out-of-state, Google and Yelp were the top tools to use
  • - users would look for bars that were likely to show the games/teams they cared about

Key Quotes:

  • “It was nice to watch the game with someone who was rooting for the same team.”
  • “If there is a game on, I look at photos for good seating and viewing. And then maybe reviews.”
  • “If I were looking for a bar for a game that night, I would look for a team bar.”

After conducting these interviews, I was able to pinpoint the app’s ideal user that would be willing to use the app.

Key Takeaways about Daniel:

  • - Prefers using websites/apps that are simple and serves its purpose
  • - Open to meeting other people/fans of the same team
  • - Loves checking up on sports news wherever and whenever

Wireframing and Prototyping

After rapidly sketching posibble layouts with a pencil and paper, I began to distill the final site layout and was able to start the process of creating wireframes, from low to medium and finally to high-fidelity. Below shows a quick visualization.

The following images are high-fidelity wireframes depicting the different screens a user would see in a typical search flow.

From the homepage, users would make a search based on the team they root for and where they're located. Alternatively, if there is an upcoming game, users would be able to choose the specific game from the homepage. Both searches would lead them to the page listing all the bars loyal to their team.

Shown below are the two different search headers used depending on how the user made their search.
